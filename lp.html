Linguistic Power

We could argue that language is a generative force and by itself is neutral. It does not simply describes reality. It is world-building and actively constructs its own reality in the minds of users and listeners. It could be easily understood that language is a potential tool with leanings either in a liberating or a destructive way. The first forms virtues, the latter manipulates.

Language can be oral or written, however, it can envoke emotion and inspire empathy for people we have never met or even for people who never existed, like characters created by great novelists such as Fyodor M. Dostoevsky. It's not an exaggeration to say that he was the dissector of human soul with his penetrating and finely crafted portrayals of both the protagonists and side characters. John R. R. Tolkien went a step further with his high fantasy by creating a fictional universe inhabited by supernatural humanoid beings.
